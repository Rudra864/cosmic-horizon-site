Black Holes

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars after supernova explosions.

Pulsars

Pulsars are rapidly spinning neutron stars that emit beams of radiation. As they rotate, these beams sweep across Earth, appearing as pulses of light.

Neutron Stars

Neutron stars are incredibly dense remnants of collapsed stars. Just a teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh billions of tons on Earth.
